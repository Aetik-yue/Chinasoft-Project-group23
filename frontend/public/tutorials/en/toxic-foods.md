# Common Toxic Foods for Parrots

Parrots metabolize food differently from people, and many foods that humans can eat are poisonous to them. This “never feed” list is worth printing and posting near the kitchen where the whole household can see it.

## Foods That Are Absolutely Prohibited

- **Avocado:** Contains persin, which is highly toxic to birds; even a small amount can be fatal.
- **Chocolate / cocoa:** Contains theobromine and can cause a rapid heart rate and seizures.
- **Coffee, tea, and energy drinks:** Contain caffeine, for which there is no safe dose.
- **Alcohol:** Even a very small amount can cause fatal poisoning.
- **High-salt foods:** Chips, salted crackers, and pickled foods can overwhelm the kidneys.
- **Snacks high in sugar or fat:** Cakes, cream, and fried foods.

## Risky Fruits and Vegetables

- **Pits and seeds from apples, pears, cherries, and similar fruit:** They contain cyanogenic compounds. The flesh may be offered, but all pits and seeds must be removed.
- **Onion, garlic, and chives:** Can damage red blood cells and cause hemolytic anemia.
- **Raw beans:** Contain phytohaemagglutinin (PHA, a plant lectin) and must be cooked thoroughly; raw green beans are especially risky.
- **Mushrooms:** Some varieties are toxic to birds, so do not take the risk.
- **Eggplant and tomato leaves and stems:** The foliage of nightshade plants contains alkaloids.
- **Burdock and rhubarb leaves:** High oxalate content can interfere with calcium absorption.

## Seasonings and Processed Foods

- Do not give parrots human meals containing salt, sugar, oil, or monosodium glutamate.
- Processed meats such as sausage, ham, and bacon contain extremely high levels of salt.
- Seasoned nuts, including salted or sugar-coated varieties, are unsafe; choose plain, additive-free nuts only.
- “Sugar-free” products may contain xylitol, which is also dangerous to pets.

## What to Do After Accidental Ingestion

1. Remove the remaining food immediately and record what was eaten, how much, and at what time.
2. Watch for vomiting, seizures, lethargy, or rapid breathing.
3. Do not induce vomiting yourself; parrots can easily aspirate material into the airway.
4. Contact an avian or exotic-pet clinic as soon as possible and bring the food packaging or any remaining food to help identify the toxin.

## Safer Treat Options

If you want to offer a treat, choose from the following:

- Plain, unsalted sunflower or hemp seeds in small amounts.
- Fresh apple slices with the seeds removed, blueberries, or diced mango.
- Cooked corn, peas, or diced carrot.
- Plain oats with no sugar or salt.

> Tip: Cooking fumes and fuel gas in the kitchen are also harmful to parrots. Move the bird to another room while cooking and run the exhaust hood.
