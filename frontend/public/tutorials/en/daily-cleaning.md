# Daily Cage Cleaning and Disinfection

A clean cage is the foundation for preventing respiratory and digestive disease. Many new owners assume that replacing food and water each day is enough, but droppings, feather dust, and food debris can all allow bacteria to multiply quickly. This guide organizes cage cleaning into daily, weekly, and monthly routines.

## Daily Essentials

Check once in the morning and once in the evening whenever possible:

- **Replace the water:** Wash the water dish thoroughly every day to prevent bacterial biofilm; in summer, replace the water twice a day if needed.
- **Clean the food dish:** Discard empty hulls and crumbs, then add fresh food.
- **Wipe the perches:** Remove droppings with a damp cloth or old toothbrush to prevent bacterial buildup.
- **Replace the cage paper or liner:** Change the tray liner every day to keep the bottom dry.
- **Inspect the toys:** Replace toys that have been chewed apart before the bird can swallow fragments.

## Weekly Deep Cleaning

Choose one day each week, move the parrot into a spare cage or travel carrier, and then:

1. Remove the food dish, water dish, perches, and toys.
2. Scrub them with warm soapy water, rinse thoroughly, and leave them to dry.
3. Wipe the cage frame and tray with a parrot-safe disinfectant.
4. Reassemble the cage only after every component is completely dry.
5. Use the opportunity to inspect the cage for rust and cracked welds.

## Monthly Maintenance

- Thoroughly rinse accumulated dirt from cage seams and crevices.
- Check perches for heavy surface wear, and sand or replace them when necessary.
- Wash or replace the cage cover.
- Check the expiration dates of food and medicines.

## Choosing a Disinfectant

| Recommended | Not recommended |
|---|---|
| F10 disinfectant | Sodium-hypochlorite household bleach |
| Diluted hypochlorous acid | Spraying alcohol directly |
| Bird-safe disinfectant | Phenolic cleaners |
| High-temperature steam | Strongly acidic or alkaline cleaners |

After disinfecting, wipe everything once more with clean water and ventilate the room for at least 30 minutes. Return the parrot only after all odors have dispersed.

## Helpful Cleaning-Order Tips

- Work from top to bottom: wipe perches and toys before cleaning the tray to avoid contaminating it a second time.
- Separate your tools: use different cloths for the cage and the food dishes to prevent cross-contamination.
- Keep two sets of food and water dishes: use one while the other dries, and alternate them each day.

> Tip: Avoid strongly scented cleaners because parrots have extremely sensitive respiratory systems. “Natural” essential oils such as lemon and tea tree are also toxic to birds.
