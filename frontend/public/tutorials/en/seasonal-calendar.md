# Year-Round Care Calendar

A parrot’s care needs change with the seasons. This 12-month checklist helps you prepare in advance and follow a steady routine, which is much easier than reacting after a problem develops.

## Spring (March–May)

- **Peak molting season:** Increase protein and calcium; see *Molting Care Essentials*.
- **Breeding season:** Watch for egg laying in females and provide an appropriate calcium source.
- **Large temperature swings:** Mornings and evenings may still be cold. Keep the bird warm and do not remove heating equipment too early.
- **More airborne allergens:** Pollen and dust increase; offer appropriate gentle misting or bathing more often for birds with sensitive airways.
- **More parasite activity:** Schedule a checkup and screen for external parasites.

## Summer (June–August)

- **Prevent overheating:** Keep the cage out of direct sun and provide bathing water; see *Summer Cooling and Winter Warmth*.
- **Food spoils quickly:** Discard moist food that has not been eaten within two hours.
- **Mosquitoes and insects:** Keep window screens secure to prevent bites that can transmit disease.
- **Humidity management:** Use a dehumidifier during prolonged rainy or humid weather.
- **Air-conditioning stress:** Never aim cold air directly at the bird, and allow time to adjust when moving between air-conditioned and outdoor spaces.

## Autumn (September–November)

- **Second molt:** Increase nutritional support again.
- **Greater temperature swings:** Respiratory illness is more common; keep the bird warm at night.
- **Seasonal appetite changes:** Monitor food intake and increase energy intake appropriately if needed.
- **Prepare for winter:** Check that heating equipment works properly.
- **Shorter daylight:** Adjust the routine and provide 10–12 hours of sleep.

## Winter (December–February)

- **Prioritize warmth:** Keep the room above 20°C and avoid cross-drafts.
- **Dry air:** Humidify if necessary, but never direct mist at the cage.
- **Reduce bathing:** If a bath is necessary, offer it around midday and make sure the bird is completely dry afterward.
- **Less activity:** Increase indoor interaction to help prevent obesity.
- **Holiday precautions:** Fireworks and crowded, noisy gatherings around Lunar New Year can be stressful; provide a quiet space.

## Monthly Tasks

- Thoroughly clean the cage and toys.
- Check cage welds, perches, and toys for safety.
- Record weight and watch the trend.
- Check expiration dates on food and medications.
- Check nail length and trim if needed.

## Weekly Tasks

- Deep-clean food and water dishes and perches.
- Rotate toys.
- Observe droppings and behavior and record anything unusual.
- Check whether the cuttlebone or mineral block needs replacement.

## Daily Tasks

- Replace water, clean the food dish, wipe perches, and change the cage liner.
- Check alertness, eating, drinking, and droppings every morning and evening.
- Keep lights and feeding on a fixed schedule.
- Provide 10–15 minutes of interaction and training.
- Weigh the bird, ideally at the same time each morning before feeding.

## Annual Tasks

- Schedule at least one avian or exotic-pet veterinary exam, including fecal testing and a complete blood count when recommended.
- Replace expired first-aid supplies.
- Inspect and update the travel carrier and heating equipment.
- Review the year’s weight and health records.

## Life-Stage Reminders

- **Young birds:** Offer smaller, more frequent meals and focus on a calm adjustment to the environment.
- **Adult birds:** Manage weight and prevent obesity.
- **Senior birds:** Reduce dietary fat, offer more easily digested foods, and arrange more frequent checkups.

> Tip: Print this calendar and place it near the cage, or set reminders on your phone. A scheduled routine is far more reliable than doing tasks only when they come to mind.
